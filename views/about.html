Welcome to moodX

Hedonimetry: the study of happiness as a measurable economic asset. It is gauged by a device called a Hedonimeter, as Francis Ysidro Edgeworth said to describe, "an ideally perfect instrument, a psychophysical machine, continually registering the height of pleasure experienced by an individual."

moodX uses deep-rich social media to track keywords in an user's post to provide accurate data on moods. The main priciple data coming from Twitter, the hedonimeter can coorelate the data from a tweet or status to measure emotional levels on graphs. The linecharts provide data 26 months from the current date along with a powerful news engine from The New York Times and DuckDuckGo.

Get Started

Hover over the line chart to see the happiness level for a paticular day. To gather more information, use The New York Times news engine to find snippets on what happened that day. For a full detailed analysis, we recommend using our DuckDuckGo engine as well.
